Biking to Hilton Tampa Downtown is possible from various parts of Tampa, given the city’s increasing support for cyclists. Below are the available options and biking routes you can use to reach the hotel.
1. Tampa Riverwalk Route
Route Overview: The Tampa Riverwalk is a popular route for both pedestrians and cyclists. It stretches along the Hillsborough River and connects various parts of downtown Tampa.
Starting Point: If you're starting from the northern parts of Tampa or near the University of Tampa, you can take the Riverwalk towards the downtown area.
Access to Hilton Tampa Downtown: Exit the Riverwalk near Kennedy Boulevard, then bike for a short distance west on Kennedy Boulevard to reach N Tampa Street, where the hotel is located.


2. Bayshore Boulevard Route
Route Overview: If you're coming from South Tampa or areas near Bayshore Boulevard, you can take the Bayshore Linear Park Trail, which is a popular cycling route.
Instructions: Follow Bayshore Boulevard north towards downtown. Once you pass the Tampa General Hospital area, cross over to the downtown area via the Platt Street Bridge.
Final Steps: After crossing the bridge, turn left onto N Tampa Street, and the hotel will be on your left.


3. Tampa Greenways and Trails
Overview: Tampa has a system of greenways and trails that make biking across different parts of the city easier.
Recommended Routes:
If you're biking from Ybor City, you can use the 7th Avenue corridor and connect to Channelside Drive. From there, head west towards downtown and connect to N Tampa Street.
If you’re starting from East Tampa, you can take the Selmon Greenway trail, which runs parallel to the Selmon Expressway and ends near downtown Tampa. You can then navigate to N Tampa Street from the terminus.


4. Bike Sharing Programs
Coast Bike Share: Tampa’s Coast Bike Share program offers a flexible option for biking to downtown from various stations across the city.
How it Works: You can rent a bike from any of the Coast Bike stations and return it to a station near Hilton Tampa Downtown.
Nearby Stations: There are several Coast Bike Share docking stations close to the hotel, particularly near the Curtis Hixon Waterfront Park.


Important Notes:
Bike Lanes: Tampa is gradually expanding its bike lanes, especially in the downtown area, so many of the roads near the hotel, such as N Tampa Street and E Kennedy Boulevard, have bike-friendly sections.
Safety: Be cautious of traffic, particularly during peak hours, as the downtown area can be busy.

4. Street Parking
Availability: There is metered parking available on nearby streets, such as E Kennedy Blvd and N Franklin St.

5. Private Parking Lots
There are smaller private lots near the hotel, which often charge flat daily rates between $5 and $15. These lots typically cater to office workers during the day but may offer better availability in the evenings and weekends.
Parking Apps:
You can also use parking apps like ParkMobile or SpotHero to reserve a spot in advance in nearby garages or lots, which can sometimes offer discounts compared to paying on-site.

1. HART Bus Service
Hillsborough Area Regional Transit (HART) operates several bus routes through downtown Tampa.
Bus Routes: Routes such as 1, 7, and 30 pass near the downtown area, including stops within walking distance of the hotel.
Cost: $2.00 per ride, or you can purchase day passes for unlimited rides.
Nearest Stops: Look for stops along E Kennedy Blvd or Franklin St, which are very close to the Hilton.


2. TECO Line Streetcar
The TECO Line Streetcar is a free service that connects downtown Tampa, Channelside, and Ybor City.
Route: You can take the streetcar from Ybor City, Channelside, or Amalie Arena and get off at the Whiting Station, which is about a 10-minute walk to the hotel.
Cost: Free to ride.
Schedule: The streetcar operates every 15 minutes during the day.


3. Downtowner Shuttle (On-Demand Service)
Downtowner is a free, on-demand shuttle service available within downtown Tampa.
How It Works: You can request a ride through their mobile app, and they will pick you up and drop you off anywhere within their downtown service area.
Cost: Free.


4. Pirate Water Taxi
The Pirate Water Taxi offers a scenic way to reach downtown Tampa by water. It stops at various locations along the Hillsborough River, including Armature Works and the Tampa Convention Center.
Cost: Around $15-$20 for an all-day pass.
Stop: The Tampa Convention Center stop is about a 10-minute walk from Hilton Tampa Downtown.


These options provide convenient and affordable alternatives to driving, whether you’re coming from other areas of Tampa or exploring the downtown region.
